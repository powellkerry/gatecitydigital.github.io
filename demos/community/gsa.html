School GSAs

Gender & Sexuality Alliances create safe, affirming spaces in schools for LGBTQ+ students and allies.

What is a GSA?

A Gender & Sexuality Alliance (GSA) is a student-run club that unites LGBTQ+ and allied youth to build community and organize around issues affecting them in school and in their communities. GSAs are protected by federal law (the Equal Access Act) and have the same rights as any other school club.

Why GSAs Matter

Students in schools with GSAs feel safer, are less likely to be bullied, and have better mental health outcomes. A GSA can be the difference between a student thriving or dropping out.

How to Start a GSA

  • Find a faculty advisor (a teacher, counselor, or staff member who supports you)
  • Submit paperwork to your school — same process as any other club
  • Connect with the GSA Network for guidance and support
  • Reach out to Sage & Rainbow — we can help!
